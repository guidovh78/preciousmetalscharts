What moves the Silver price

Silver is both a monetary metal and an industrial one: roughly half of demand comes from industry — solar panels, electronics and electrical contacts. That dual role, on a much smaller market than gold, makes silver more volatile. The gold-to-silver ratio is the classic gauge of how cheap or dear silver is relative to gold.

Common questions

What is the live silver price?

The live silver price here is the spot price per troy ounce in US dollars, aggregated from public market data and ~10 minutes delayed. Per-gram and per-kilo prices are shown automatically.

Why is silver more volatile than gold?

Silver has a much smaller market than gold and around half its demand is industrial (solar, electronics). Industrial swings on top of investment demand make its price move more sharply in both directions.

What is the silver price per kilo?

Multiply the per-gram price by 1,000, or the troy-ounce price by 32.1507. We show the per-kilo price automatically above.

What is the gold-to-silver ratio?

It is the gold price divided by the silver price — the number of ounces of silver it takes to buy one ounce of gold. A high ratio is widely read as silver being cheap relative to gold. See our live ratio page.
