02

The three main ways to buy

Each route trades off cost, control and convenience differently. There is no single "best" — only the best fit for what you want.

In hand

Physical coins & bars

You own and hold the metal. Highest premiums (largest on small silver coins), plus shipping and the cost and risk of storing or insuring it yourself. Best if possession matters most to you.

Lowest cost

Vaulted / allocated

You own specific metal stored in a professional vault, bought and sold at a small margin over spot. Cheapest to trade and easy to resell — but the metal stays in the vault, not your hands.

Little & often

Savings plans

Automated monthly buying that accumulates fractional metal over time. Convenient for steady saving; check the ongoing fee and whether you can take delivery or sell back easily.

04

Common questions

Is it better to buy physical or vaulted gold?

Physical coins and bars give you metal in hand but carry higher premiums and the cost and risk of storing it yourself. Vaulted (allocated) gold is bought at a small margin over spot and stored in a professional vault — cheaper to trade, but you don't hold the metal directly. The right choice depends on how much you value possession versus low cost and easy resale.

Why is the price I pay higher than spot?

Physical metal trades above the spot reference because of the premium — a markup covering minting, fabrication, distribution and dealer margin. Premiums are usually larger on small coins than large bars, and larger on silver than on gold.

How do I compare two dealers fairly?

Compare the all-in premium over spot, the buy–sell spread, storage and shipping, minimum order, payment methods, where they ship, and how the metal is held or insured. A low headline price with high shipping or a wide spread is not actually cheap.
