What moves the Gold price

The gold price is driven mainly by monetary factors: the US dollar, real interest rates and inflation expectations, plus central-bank buying and safe-haven demand in times of stress. Unlike silver or platinum, gold has limited industrial use, so it moves more on macro sentiment than on factory demand.

Common questions

What is the live gold price?

The live gold price on this page is the spot price per troy ounce in US dollars, aggregated from public market data and refreshed about every 10 minutes. It is a reference, not a real-time trading price.

What is the gold price per gram?

Divide the troy-ounce price by 31.1035. We show the per-gram and per-kilo price automatically above; one troy ounce of gold equals 31.1035 grams.

Why do dealers charge more than the spot gold price?

Physical coins and bars cost more than spot because of the premium — the markup for minting, fabrication, distribution and dealer margin. Premiums are larger on small coins than on large bars.
