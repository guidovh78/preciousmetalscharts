What moves the Platinum price

Platinum is largely an industrial metal: its biggest use is in autocatalysts (especially diesel), alongside jewellery and industry. Supply is concentrated in South Africa, so strikes or power cuts can move the price. Because demand is cyclical, platinum sometimes trades below gold despite being rarer.

Common questions

What is the live platinum price?

The live platinum price here is the spot price per troy ounce in US dollars, aggregated from public market data and ~10 minutes delayed, with per-gram and per-kilo shown automatically.

Why is platinum sometimes cheaper than gold?

Platinum is rarer than gold but its price is driven more by industrial demand (especially diesel autocatalysts). When that demand is weak, platinum can trade below gold even though it is scarcer.

What drives the platinum price?

Mainly autocatalyst and industrial demand, plus supply from a small number of mines concentrated in South Africa and Russia. Supply disruptions can move the price sharply.

What is the platinum price per gram?

Divide the troy-ounce price by 31.1035. The per-gram and per-kilo prices are shown automatically above.
