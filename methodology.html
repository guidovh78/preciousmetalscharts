Methodology & data sources

Last updated 28 June 2026

How preciousmetalscharts sources, calculates and updates the data on this site — so you can judge it for yourself.

Where the prices come from

Live spot prices for gold, silver, platinum and palladium are aggregated from public market-data sources and refreshed automatically about every 10 minutes. We label them “~10 min delayed” — a reference, not a real-time trading feed. The pipeline uses multiple sources with automatic failover and sanity-range validation, so one broken feed can’t put a wrong number live; if every source fails, the last good value is kept and flagged.

Historical data

The long-term charts are built from our own price archive. Deep history is seeded from the World Bank Commodity Price Data (“The Pink Sheet”), used under CC BY 4.0, which provides monthly gold, silver and platinum prices back to the 1970s and earlier. Recent years are daily. The archive is appended once a day and stored as static files, so it stays ours and stays consistent.

How the gold-to-silver ratio is calculated

The ratio is simply the gold price divided by the silver price, both per troy ounce. Because it is a ratio of two prices in the same currency, the number is the same whether you measure in dollars or euros.

Units

Precious metals are priced per troy ounce, which equals 31.1035 grams — heavier than the everyday (avoirdupois) ounce of 28.35 g. A per-gram price is the troy-ounce price divided by 31.1035.

What “spot price” means

The spot price is the current reference price to buy or sell one troy ounce of unfabricated metal for immediate settlement. Physical coins and bars cost more than spot, because dealers add a premium for minting, fabrication, distribution and margin.

How we compare providers

On our buying guide, providers are ranked on a fixed set of criteria — all-in cost (40%), trust & transparency (25%), access (20%) and liquidity (15%) — weighted before any commercial relationship is considered. Commissions never change the order.
