Is a gold IRA right for you?

The honest two-sided view — the part most dealer-owned sites skip.

It can make sense if…

  • You want a tangible hedge against inflation inside a retirement wrapper.
  • You're diversifying a portion of a larger portfolio — not betting it all on metal.
  • You're rolling over an old 401(k) and want tax-deferred metal exposure.
  • You plan to hold long term and can absorb short-term swings.

Think twice if…

  • Costs matter most: fees plus coin markups make these pricier than an ETF.
  • You want income: physical metal pays no dividend or interest.
  • Home storage is a myth: the IRS requires an approved depository.
  • Watch the markup: some firms push high-premium "collectible" coins.

Common questions

A self-directed IRA that holds IRS-approved physical precious metals — gold, silver, platinum or palladium — instead of, or alongside, paper assets. It carries the same tax treatment as a traditional or Roth IRA; the difference is the underlying asset.

You open a self-directed IRA with a custodian, then transfer funds from an existing 401(k) or IRA. A direct (trustee-to-trustee) rollover moves the money without it passing through your hands, avoiding taxes and penalties. The provider's specialist usually handles the paperwork with your old plan.

Expect a one-time setup fee, an annual custodian fee, and an annual storage fee at an approved depository — often a few hundred dollars a year combined. On top of that, dealers add a premium over spot when you buy the metal. Always ask for the all-in cost before committing.

No. The IRS requires metals in an IRA to be held by an approved trustee at a qualified depository. Any pitch for a 'home-storage gold IRA' should be treated as a serious warning sign — it can disqualify the account and trigger taxes and penalties.

Storage and custody are highly secure and insured. The real risk is market risk: metal prices move, and physical metal pays no yield. Treat it as one diversifying slice of a retirement plan rather than a guaranteed store of growth.
